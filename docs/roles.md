# User Roles and Dashboard Capabilities

This document outlines the primary user roles within the Crossview College of Theology and Technology LMS and details the information and capabilities relevant to their respective dashboards.

## 1. Student Role

**Dashboard View:**
*   Enrolled courses and progress percentage in each.
*   Upcoming lessons, quizzes, or assignments.
*   Personalized course recommendations based on activity and interests.
*   Achievements: Display of earned badges, points, and current leaderboard ranking (if gamification features are active).
*   Current subscription tier, benefits, and options to upgrade or manage the subscription.
*   Notifications: Alerts for new course content, submission deadlines, quiz results, messages from teachers (if implemented), and system announcements.

**Key Actions & Access Rights:**
*   Browse and search the public course catalog.
*   View detailed information for any course: curriculum, learning objectives, teacher profile, pricing, and required subscription tier.
*   Enroll in courses either through an active subscription (if tier requirements are met) or via direct course purchase.
*   Access and interact with all content (lessons, videos, documents, quizzes, assignments) within their enrolled courses.
*   Submit assignments and take quizzes.
*   View their own quiz scores, feedback, and attempt history.
*   Manage their personal user profile, including contact information and password.
*   If applicable, generate unique invite codes for parents to link accounts for monitoring purposes.
*   View their enrollment history and payment/subscription history.

## 2. Teacher Role

**Dashboard View:**
*   A list of all courses they have created and are managing.
*   For each of their courses:
    *   Total number of enrolled students.
    *   Aggregate student progress summary (e.g., average completion rate).
    *   Key analytics: enrollment trends, student engagement metrics, average quiz scores.
*   Quick access links/buttons to create a new course or edit existing ones.
*   Student Progress Monitoring Section:
    *   A list of students enrolled in each of their courses.
    *   Ability to select a specific student to view their detailed progress within that course (lesson completion status, quiz attempts and scores).
*   Course Analytics Section:
    *   Overall enrollment trends across all their courses.
    *   Performance metrics for individual courses (e.g., completion rates, student feedback if available).
*   Compensation & Payouts Section:
    *   Summary of estimated or accrued earnings based on direct purchases and subscription models.
    *   History of past payouts.
*   Notifications: Alerts for new student enrollments in their courses, submitted assignments awaiting grading, questions from students (if Q&A features are active).

**Key Actions & Access Rights:**
*   Create new courses, including defining title, description, learning objectives, target audience (category, subject, grade level), cover image, pricing, and any required subscription tier.
*   Edit, update, and manage all aspects of their own courses.
*   Build and organize course curriculum: create sections, add lessons (supporting various content types like text, video, PDF, external links), create quizzes, and design assignments.
*   Upload and manage course materials and resources.
*   Publish or unpublish their courses.
*   View and grade student assignment submissions, providing feedback.
*   Monitor student progress and performance within their own courses.
*   Access analytics and reports related *only* to their own courses and enrolled students.
*   Manage their payment details to receive compensation.
*   Can view the content of their own courses without needing a formal enrollment.

## 3. Parent Role

**Dashboard View:**
*   A list of their linked student(s). If multiple students are linked, an option to select and switch between individual student views.
*   For each selected linked student:
    *   A summary of courses the student is currently enrolled in.
    *   The student's current progress percentage in each of those courses.
    *   Highlights of recent activity, such as recently completed lessons or quiz scores.
    *   Attendance records (if this feature is implemented and tracked by the school/teachers).
*   Notifications: Alerts specifically related to their linked student(s), such as course completion milestones, new course enrollments, exceptionally low or high quiz scores, or messages from teachers intended for parents.

**Key Actions & Access Rights:**
*   Manage connections to their children's student accounts (e.g., enter an invite code, accept a link request, view currently linked students, and request to unlink).
*   View the academic progress, list of enrolled courses, grades, and activity reports *only* for their explicitly linked student(s).
*   Cannot directly access or interact with course content (e.g., take lessons or quizzes) on behalf of the student.
*   Manage their own user profile, including contact details and notification preferences for alerts related to their child.

## 4. Admin Role

*(Note: While admins might have a master dashboard, their capabilities are generally platform-wide and not restricted in the same way as other roles. This summary is for contextual understanding).*

**Dashboard View (Conceptual):**
*   Platform-wide analytics: total users by role, total courses, active enrollments, revenue metrics, system health indicators.
*   Quick access to user management, course management, subscription tier configuration, payout processing, and system settings.
*   Lists of pending approvals or items requiring attention (e.g., reported content, teacher applications if applicable).

**Key Actions & Access Rights:**
*   Full Create, Read, Update, Delete (CRUD) capabilities across all major entities: users (all roles), courses, categories, subjects, grade levels, subscription tiers, etc.
*   Manage user roles and permissions.
*   Can typically bypass content access restrictions (e.g., view any course content for quality assurance or support purposes).
*   Configure platform settings, payment gateways, and compensation rules.
*   Facilitate parent-student account linking if manual intervention is required.
*   Oversee and process teacher payouts.
*   Monitor system logs and handle administrative tasks.
